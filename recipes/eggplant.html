Fried eggplant

Fried eggplant recipe

Slices of fresh eggplant coated in a seasoned breadcrumb or flour mixture, fried to a crispy golden brown,
resulting in a delectable dish with a crispy exterior and tender,
flavorful interior that pairs well with a variety of sauces or can be enjoyed on its own.

Ingredients

  • Eggplant (medium-sized)
  • Salt
  • All-purpose flour or breadcrumbs
  • Eggs
  • Seasonings
  • Vegetable oil

Steps

  1. Start by preparing the eggplant. Wash the eggplant and trim off the ends. Then, slice the eggplant into even rounds or lengthwise into strips, depending on your preference.
  2. Place the eggplant slices on a paper towel-lined tray or cutting board. Sprinkle salt over both sides of the eggplant slices and let them sit for about 15-20 minutes.
  3. After the resting time, pat the eggplant slices dry with paper towels to remove the released moisture.
  4. In a shallow bowl or dish, beat the eggs. In a separate bowl or plate, add the flour or breadcrumbs.
  5. Dip each eggplant slice into the beaten eggs, making sure it is coated on both sides. Allow any excess egg to drip off.
  6. Next, coat the eggplant slice in the flour or breadcrumb mixture, ensuring that it is fully coated on all sides. Gently press the coating onto the eggplant to help it adhere.
  7. Repeat the dipping and coating process for each eggplant slice.
  8. Heat vegetable oil in a frying pan or skillet over medium heat. The oil should be hot enough for frying, but not smoking.
  9. Carefully place the coated eggplant slices into the hot oil, leaving space between them to ensure even cooking.
  10. Fry the eggplant slices for a few minutes on each side until they turn golden brown and become crispy. Flip them with a spatula or tongs to cook evenly.
  11. Once fried, transfer the cooked eggplant slices to a paper towel-lined plate or tray to drain excess oil.
  12. Repeat the frying process with the remaining eggplant slices.
